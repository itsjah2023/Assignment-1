Three Everyday Usability Problems

1. Celing Fans

What the problem is: Some main issues found in celing fans are that sometime make alot of noise (humming, grinding) and they sometimes the blades are wobbly; which can lead to balde falling off and cause serious injuries

Which usability dimensions suffer: Learnability — New people who use celing fans will have a hard to to understand how the controls will work. Efficiency — a long time to figure out the controls Safety — Not knowing how the controls work will cause the fan to broke or balde to fly off which will cause injury

Suggested fix: Tighten all screws and blade attachments, Clean the fan and oil the motor, Make sure fan direction is set properly

2. Gas-power Stove

What the problem is: Many gas power stove lose a lot of heat in the air, making it less sufficent that electric and induction stove, as gas prices fluctuate, costs can rise.

Which usability dimensions suffer: Learnability — new users must test how to turn on and off the stove and also make sure its fully on. Efficiency — Repeated process can waste gas. Safety — burning natural gas can cause carbon monoxide which can cause irritated lungs.

Suggested fix: Install a carbon monoxide dectector near the kicthen, regularly check for gas leaks.

3. Air Conditioner

What the problem is: Air Conditioners can circulate dust, mold, or bacteria if filters aren’t cleaned regularly, Electricity bills can be expensive.

Which usability dimensions suffer: Learnability — installtion can be hard and someone professional would have to be hired. Efficiency — waste of time and money Safety — using air conditioner for too long can cause irritation of the eyes and skin and cause repistatory infection.

Suggested fix: Use inveter AC with high Energy Star Rating, clean or change out AC fiters once a month.
